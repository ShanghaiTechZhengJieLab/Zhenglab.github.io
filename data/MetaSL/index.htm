Supplements of the manuscript:

In Silico Prediction of Synthetic Lethality by Meta-Analysis of Genetic Interactions, Functions and Pathways in Yeast and Human Cancer

Min Wu, Xuejuan Li, Fan Zhang, Xiaoli Li, Chee-Keong Kwoh and Jie Zheng

Introduction

A pair of genes is called synthetic lethality (SL) if mutations of both genes will kill a cell while mutation of either gene alone will not.

The following figure demonstrates the above concept of synthetic lethality (the figure is taken from [1]). Hence, a gene in SL interactions with a cancer-specific mutated gene will be a promising drug target with anti-cancer selectivity.

 

Method

Our approach MetaSL to predicting SL in yeast and human cancer consists of two stages.

 

First, we integrate 17 genomic and proteomic features and the outputs of 10 classification methods to predicted yeast SL pairs.

 

Second, we designed various orthologous mappings between human and yeast genes, and then we identified SL in human cancers via knowledge transfer from yeast to human.
